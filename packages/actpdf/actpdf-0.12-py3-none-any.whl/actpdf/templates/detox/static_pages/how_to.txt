<p>This report is presented in a user friendly language and format. The following tips will help you get the best information value out of the report.</p>

<p><b>1. What does the term "Average/Intermediate" mean in the report?</b></p>
<p>Average implies neither high nor low, rather an moderate outcome. For example, moderately increased/decreased CYP1A1 activity means, the activity of this enzyme varies slightly above or below the normal activity. <p>
<p><b>2. How do I know which result is applicable to me?</b></p>
<p>Only results with a check mark <i class="positive checkmark icon"></i> are applicable to you, the others are not applicable. All possible outcomes are provided in the table to provide a context to your outcome.</p>
<p><b>3. Where did the information contained in the report come from?</b></p>
<p>The genetic markers that are used in this report are based on scientific studies published in international journals. A list of references is available for you to read on our web blog.</p>
<p><b>4. Some sentences are colored in green and others in red, why?</b></p>
<p>Attributes that are advantageous to the detoxification process are indicated in green and those that are not advantageous are in red. Moderate or Neutral outcomes are indicated in orange.</p>
